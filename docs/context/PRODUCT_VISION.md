# Product vision — industry-standard workout tracker

**North star**: Help users train consistently, progress measurably, and understand **what to do next** — without replacing a coach or medical advice.

## Pillars

1. **Structure** — Users start from **curated, best-in-class templates** (push/pull/legs, 5×5, upper/lower, common accessories) or define **custom** workouts and exercises.
2. **Progressive overload** — The app is opinionated about **tracking load × volume × frequency** over time so users (and the UI) can see trends, plateaus, and PRs.
3. **Guidance** — Suggest **sets, reps, RPE/RIR, rest, and next-session targets** from history and simple rules (e.g., “add 2.5 lb when all sets hit rep target at RPE ≤ 7”). Suggestions are **editable** and clearly labeled as **algorithmic**, not medical.
4. **Long-term usability** — Fast logging during training, review on rest days, exports later (roadmap), minimal friction on mobile-width viewports.

## User flows (target)

- Pick a **program** or **session template** → pre-filled exercises → adjust → train → log sets with **one-tap** where possible.
- **Custom** workout: add exercises, save as reusable template.
- After a session: see **summary**, **vs last time**, and **next session suggestion** (weight/reps/sets).
- Optional: **deload** weeks, **injury** flags per movement (reduce load suggestions).

## Safety and compliance

- Show a fixed **disclaimer**: not medical advice; consult professionals for injury or conditions.
- No shame language; encourage sustainable progression.

## Out of scope for early milestones

- Real-time social feed, competitive leaderboards (unless explicitly prioritized later).
- Proprietary exercise video hosting (linking to reputable external resources is fine).
